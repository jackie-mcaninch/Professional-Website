About Me

Hi, my name is Jackie and I am a 4th-Year Computer Science Major studying at Illinois Institute of Technology. I will obtain my bachelor's degree by May 2023, and I plan to earn my master's degree in Data Science by December 2023. Currently, I am looking for internships and research opportunities for the summer of 2023 that can translate into a full-time position after graduation. I prefer in-person work and am willing to relocate if the position requires it.

Regarding my career-oriented interests, some of my favorite classes in computer science so far have been Systems Programming (351), Introduction to Algorithms (430), Data-Intensive Computing (554), and Machine Learning (584). In my time researching at IIT, I've developed an interest in system resource management, algorithm efficiency, and low-level device programming. Looking forward, however, I am looking to master the application of data science and machine learning, along with other use cases of big data. I've always been passionate about cryptocurrency, and my latest industry experience allowed me to get involved in the world of blockchain and open source development, which you can read more about on my Projects page. Someday, as it is a direct threat to the encryption involved in blockchain infrastructure, I would love to delve into the field of quantum computing, something of which I find wildly intriguing to read about but don't yet have the physics background to fully understand.

Campus Involvement

I am currently vice president of ACM-W, our school's female branch of Association of Computing Machinery. It is my fourth year on the executive board and I just love being involved in the club! Some of my responsibilities nowadays include communicating to our members through mass email, training new exec members, facilitating events, and marketing to the student body. We host a variety of workshops, panels, and social events, all which benefit the women of IIT by fostering a community of professional, successful, and supportive peers. I love to stay after our workshops to have conversations with women wanting to get involved with the club, and it has been so rewarding to watch the club grow and prosper over the last few years!

Another one of my favorite ways I've gotten involved at the university is through my position as a teaching assistant for our data structures and algorithms class. I find TAing to be super rewarding as I can share the knowledge I've learned with students who need it to grow in their careers as well. Preparing demos for these students has also helped me personally develop my understanding of data structures and algorithms, as well as sharpen my communication and presentation skills. Many students come to me feeling overwhelmed in the class, and I really do gain a lot of satisfaction in taking the time to help them master the topics.
